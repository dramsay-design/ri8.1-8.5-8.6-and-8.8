Main Text

Paragraph 1. Schools across the United States are facing a new challenge as social media continues to influence students' lives. One platform that has grown extremely popular among teenagers is TikTok, an app where users watch and create short videos. While the platform can encourage creativity, many educators believe younger students may not yet have the maturity needed to manage its risks responsibly.

Paragraph 2. Researchers studying adolescent development explain that the teenage brain is still learning how to manage emotions, decision making, and long-term thinking. Because of this, students sometimes struggle to recognize how online actions can affect their future opportunities. When videos are shared publicly, they can spread quickly and remain online for years.

Paragraph 3. Mental health experts have also raised concerns about how constant social media use affects students’ well-being. Studies reported by the American Psychological Association show that heavy social media use is linked to increased anxiety, poor sleep, and reduced concentration in school. TikTok’s design encourages users to continue scrolling by automatically recommending new videos based on previous viewing habits.

Paragraph 4. Another concern involves misinformation. Many TikTok videos share opinions or dramatic claims without providing reliable evidence. Younger students who are still learning how to evaluate sources may believe misleading content. As a result, educators emphasize the importance of developing strong media literacy skills before participating heavily on social platforms.

Paragraph 5. Online safety is another factor experts discuss when debating age limits for social media use. Some reports from the Pew Research Center reveal that many teenagers experience online harassment or uncomfortable interactions with strangers. Because TikTok allows users to comment, share videos, and send messages, younger students may face situations they are not prepared to manage responsibly.

Paragraph 6. For these reasons, some educators suggest that students should wait until age sixteen before using TikTok regularly. They argue that by this age, adolescents have developed stronger critical thinking skills and emotional maturity. Waiting may allow students to enjoy technology while also protecting their well-being and safety.

RI.8.1

What the standard says: Cite the textual evidence that most strongly supports an analysis of what the text says explicitly as well as inferences drawn from the text.

Reminder

This standard asks you to find the strongest evidence in the text. Sometimes the answer is directly stated. Sometimes you must make an inference, but your inference still must be supported by the text.

Thinking Hint
Pause, reread, and ask: Which exact words in the paragraph prove the answer?
Look for signal words such as linked to, because, and as a result. These often point to evidence you can use.

Modeled Example 1: Vocabulary in Context

EOG-Type Question: As used in paragraph 4, what does the word misleading most nearly mean?

Answer Choices:

A. amusing and entertaining
B. likely to confuse or give the wrong idea
C. fully proven by research
D. too difficult to understand

How to answer using the text: Read the sentences around the word. Paragraph 4 explains that some TikTok videos share opinions or dramatic claims without reliable evidence. Then it says younger students may believe that content. That context shows that misleading means something that can fool a person or lead them to a wrong belief.

Correct Answer: B. likely to confuse or give the wrong idea

Modeled Example 2: Inference

EOG-Type Question: Which inference is best supported by paragraph 3?

Answer Choices:

A. Students who use TikTok will always fail their classes.
B. Long periods of scrolling may make it harder for some students to stay focused on school responsibilities.
C. Teachers want all social media banned permanently.
D. Anxiety is caused only by homework, not by social media.

How to answer using the text: Paragraph 3 explains that heavy social media use is linked to anxiety, poor sleep, and reduced concentration in school. It also says TikTok keeps users scrolling through recommended videos. Those details support the inference that long use may interfere with focus and school responsibilities. The text does not say every student will fail, so A is too extreme.

Correct Answer: B. Long periods of scrolling may make it harder for some students to stay focused on school responsibilities.

RI.8.5

What the standard says: Analyze in detail the structure of a specific paragraph in a text, including the role of particular sentences in developing and refining a key concept.

Reminder

This standard asks you to study how a paragraph is built and how its sentences work together to develop the author’s ideas.

Thinking Hint
Ask: What job is this paragraph doing in the text?
Good readers notice whether a paragraph introduces a new concern, adds evidence, gives an example, or explains why something matters.

How to answer using the text: Paragraph 4 begins with “Another concern involves misinformation.” That tells you the paragraph is adding a new supporting reason. The rest of the paragraph explains why misinformation is dangerous for younger users. That means the paragraph develops the larger argument by adding another reason for caution.

Correct Answer: A. It introduces a new concern about misinformation and explains why younger users may struggle to judge online content.

RI.8.6

What the standard says: Determine an author’s point of view or purpose in a text and analyze how the author acknowledges and responds to conflicting evidence or viewpoints.

Reminder

This standard asks you to determine what the author believes and how the author responds to other viewpoints or possible objections.

Thinking Hint
Look for places where the author admits a benefit, then shifts back to a concern.
Signal words such as while, but, and however often show how an author acknowledges another viewpoint before responding to it.

Modeled Example 1: Point of View

EOG-Type Question: Which statement best expresses the author’s point of view?

Answer Choices:

A. TikTok should be used in every classroom because it is creative.
B. Younger students may not be ready to use TikTok responsibly because of several risks.
C. All technology should be removed from teenagers’ lives.
D. Social media has no meaningful influence on adolescents.

How to answer using the text: Look at the full passage, especially the last paragraph. The author presents concerns about mental health, misinformation, online safety, and maturity. The conclusion states that some educators suggest students should wait until age sixteen. That shows the author’s point of view is that younger students may not yet be ready to use TikTok responsibly.

Correct Answer: B. Younger students may not be ready to use TikTok responsibly because of several risks.

RI.8.8

What the standard says: Delineate and evaluate the argument and specific claims in a text, assessing whether the reasoning is sound and the evidence is relevant and sufficient.

Reminder

This standard asks you to identify the claim, trace the reasons, and judge whether the evidence truly supports the argument.

Thinking Hint
Ask: What is the claim, and which details actually support it?
Strong evidence is directly connected to the author’s claim. Irrelevant details may sound related, but they do not prove the point.

Modeled Example 1: Identifying the Claim and Reasons

EOG-Type Question: Which statement best identifies the author’s main claim?

Answer Choices:

A. Teenagers should spend more time on TikTok to improve their creativity.
B. Students should wait until age sixteen before using TikTok regularly.
C. Social media companies should stop creating short videos.
D. Mental health experts disagree completely about social media.

How to answer using the text: Read the conclusion in paragraph 6. It clearly states that some educators suggest students should wait until age sixteen before using TikTok regularly. The earlier paragraphs provide reasons to support that claim, such as mental health, misinformation, and online safety concerns.

Correct Answer: B. Students should wait until age sixteen before using TikTok regularly.

New Practice Text

Social media has become a powerful part of everyday life for teenagers, but not all platforms are appropriate for younger users. One of the most popular platforms among adolescents is TikTok, an app where users watch and create short videos. While the platform can be entertaining and creative, many educators and child development experts argue that students under the age of 16 may not yet have the maturity needed to handle its risks responsibly. Because of concerns about mental health, misinformation, and online safety, many believe students should not use TikTok until they are at least sixteen years old.

Research has shown that heavy social media use can affect teenagers’ mental health. According to studies discussed by the American Psychological Association, adolescents who spend excessive time on social media may experience increased anxiety, depression, and sleep problems. TikTok’s algorithm is designed to keep users watching videos for long periods of time by constantly recommending new content. As a result, many students lose track of time and spend hours scrolling instead of focusing on schoolwork, physical activity, or healthy sleep habits.

Another concern is that younger students often struggle to recognize misleading or false information online. Experts from Common Sense Media explain that adolescents are still developing critical thinking skills needed to evaluate the accuracy of online content. TikTok videos can spread rumors, unverified claims, or dangerous challenges very quickly because millions of users can share them within minutes. Without strong media literacy skills, younger teens may believe or imitate harmful trends before realizing the risks.

Online safety is another reason many people support limiting access to TikTok for younger students. Reports from Pew Research Center indicate that many teenagers have experienced some form of online harassment or unwanted contact on social media platforms. TikTok allows strangers to comment on videos, send messages, and share personal content widely. Younger adolescents may not yet understand how to protect their privacy or respond safely when negative interactions occur online.

In addition, the design of TikTok encourages constant engagement through likes, comments, and follower counts. These features can create pressure for students to compare themselves to others or seek approval from strangers. Researchers studying adolescent development note that teenagers are especially sensitive to social feedback during middle school years. Waiting until age sixteen gives students more time to develop confidence and emotional maturity before participating in highly public online environments.

Supporters of a higher age limit argue that delaying access would not eliminate creativity or communication. Students could still use educational platforms, school technology tools, and supervised media to learn digital skills. By the time students reach sixteen, they are typically better able to manage their time, think critically about information, and understand the long-term consequences of their online actions.

For these reasons, many educators and researchers believe setting an age limit of sixteen for TikTok would help protect younger students. Evidence from psychology organizations, media researchers, and online safety experts shows that adolescents are still developing the responsibility needed to navigate complex social media environments. Waiting until students are older could reduce exposure to harmful content while allowing them to build the maturity required to use technology safely and responsibly.
